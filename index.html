About me

Digging deeper into
soft robots ✦

I'm a PhD student at Yale's Faboratory, advised by Prof. Rebecca Kramer-Bottiglio. I research burrowing robots — designing systems that mimic the compliance and adaptability of biological organisms. My work sits at the intersection of materials science, mechanical engineering, and embodied intelligence.

Outside the lab, I run a one-person ice cream kitchen, chasing bold flavor combinations with the same curiosity I bring to robot design.

Research

Burrowing robots.

Soft burrowing robotic systems at the Faboratory, advised by Prof. Rebecca Kramer-Bottiglio.

🥼

Common Ground: Standardizing Granular Media Characteristics for Burrowing Robots

We propose standardized metrics to characterize granular media testbeds. Understanding how soil properties affect soil state — and thus the required burrowing forces — can help identify and evaluate suitable burrowing methods for specific granular environments.

🦑

Grand Challenges for Burrowing Soft Robots

This review explores the role of soft materials in biological burrowing and their implications for robotic design. We analyze gaps between natural burrowers and soft robotic burrowers, identify grand challenges, and propose opportunities to enhance burrowing performance.
