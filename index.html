About me

Digging deeper into
soft robots.

I'm a PhD student researching burrowing robots — designing systems that mimic the compliance, adaptability, and elegance of biological organisms. My work sits at the intersection of materials science, mechanical engineering, and embodied intelligence.

When I'm not in the lab, I'm in the kitchen experimenting with fun ice cream flavors: matcha ice cream with red bean paste and butter mochi, corn ice cream with corn bread and honey, hot chocolate ice cream with cinnamon, marshmallows, and peppermint. Food science isn't so different from robot design — both are about understanding how materials behave.

Research

Burrowing robots.

My work focuses on designing bioinspired soft robotic systems for burrowing in granular media.

🥼

Common Ground: Standardizing Granular Media Characteristics for Burrowing Robots

we propose standardized metrics that could be reported to characterize granular media testbeds. Understanding how soil properties affect soil state—and thus the required burrowing forces—can help identify and evaluate suitable burrowing methods employed by soft robots for specific granular environments.

Grand Challenges for Burrowing Soft Robots
